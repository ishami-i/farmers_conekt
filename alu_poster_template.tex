\block{1. Problem, Context, and Users}{
\takeawaybox{Start with the engineering problem and why solving it matters.}

\textbf{Use 3--4 lines only.} State the context, the affected users, the practical pain point, and the consequence of leaving it unsolved.

\begin{itemize}
  \item What environment are you designing for?
  \item What exactly is broken, slow, costly, unsafe, or inaccessible?
  \item Who is most affected, and under what constraints?
  \item Why is this problem worth solving now?
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Recommended pattern:} In \underline{context}, \underline{users} struggle with \underline{problem}. Existing approaches fall short because \underline{gap}. We designed \underline{solution} to improve \underline{outcome}.
}

\block{2. Objectives and Scope}{
Keep this section tight and measurable.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Aim:} Build and evaluate a system for \dots
  \item \textbf{Objective 1:} Design the workflow / architecture.
  \item \textbf{Objective 2:} Implement the prototype or model.
  \item \textbf{Objective 3:} Test usability, performance, or reliability.
  \item \textbf{Scope:} What is included now, and what is not?
\end{itemize}
}

\block{4. Constraints and Design Decisions}{
Posters are stronger when they show real engineering trade-offs.

\begin{itemize}
  \item Connectivity, power, budget, or hardware constraints
  \item Data quality, privacy, security, or compliance issues
  \item Why you chose this architecture, model, stack, or device setup
  \item Main trade-off: what you optimized for, and what you sacrificed
\end{itemize}
}

\block{8. Impact, Contribution, and Learning}{
Frame the contribution at three levels.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Technical:} What did you build, optimize, or integrate?
  \item \textbf{User:} What becomes easier, faster, safer, cheaper, or more inclusive?
  \item \textbf{Research:} What did you learn, validate, or discover?
  \item \textbf{Mission:} Why does this matter in an ALU / African context?
\end{itemize}

\takeawaybox{Do not just show that it works. Show why it matters.}
}

\block{9. Limitations and Next Steps}{
Strong projects acknowledge what is still unfinished.

\begin{itemize}
  \item Limitations in data, hardware, testing scope, or deployment
  \item Assumptions and known failure points
  \item Immediate next iteration toward real-world use
  \item Long-term future work or scaling path
\end{itemize}
}
